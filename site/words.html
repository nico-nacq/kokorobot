Words, expressions....

  • 水母の骨, くらげのほね or "Jellyfish bones". And expression to mean something that one would not expect to exist, something exceedingly rare, like jellyfish bones.
  • Pleurir. A combination of two French words, "pleurer", to cry, and "rire" to laugh. Pleurir means "laugh-cry", which could be interpreted to mean that something is so funny that it makes you cry, or that something is so absurdly sad that it makes you laugh.
  • Mots D'Heures: Gousses, Rames. A French mondegreen (pronounced with a strong French accent) of an English sentence, to say "Mother Goose Rhymes". You really have to master the art of NOT speaking English very well to fully appreciate this.

Fun with words

  • Mondegreen — a mishearing or misinterpretation of a phrase in a way that gives it a new meaning.
  • Malapropism — the mistaken use of an incorrect word in place of a word with a similar sound, resulting in a nonsensical, sometimes humorous utterance.
  • Spoonerism — an error in speech in which corresponding consonants, vowels, or morphemes are switched (see metathesis) between two words in a phrase.
