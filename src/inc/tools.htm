Because I don't always have a lot of power available on the boat I have a two workstations, one for regular use and the other for low-power situations.

I draw by hand a lot, and digitize the drawings by photographing them (I don't own a scanner) and cleaning them up in Gimp (threshold). Most of my art nowadays are line drawings, which take up less space, and are easier to process with simple tools. I like to dither (using didder) my art to save space, but also because I like the look.

Low-power workstation

I use Obi, a Macbook Air 2013, it presently runs the community version of Manjaro(i3), the laptop was donated to Hundred Rabbits by Lloyd Williams. This computer draws 45w per hour.

Accessories:

  • Wacom intuos 4 tablet
  • Bluetooth mouse

Low-power writing workstation

I write using Left on my Raspberry Pi.

  • Raspberry Pi 3B+
  • Raspberry Pi Touch 7", 800x480
  • SmartPi Touch 7" Case
  • 32GB usb stick
  • TADA68 mechanical keyboard
  • Bluetooth mouse

Regular workstation

When I have access to shore power, I use Vincent, an old 2010 Macbook Pro with the ElementaryOS Linux operating system, a generous donation by my friend John Eternal.

It draws more power than Obi, and the battery is mostly dead (it has 5 minutes of power independence). I've replaced the battery twice, the first one was a third-party battery and it lasted a year, the second was an ifixit battery and its performance has also died quick.

Accessories:

  • Wacom intuos 4 tablet
  • Bluetooth mouse
